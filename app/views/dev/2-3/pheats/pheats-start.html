About the scheme

The plant health exports audited trader scheme (PHEATS) is for people sending fruit, vegetables or cut flowers from Great Britain to the EU or Northern Ireland. If you register an address for PHEATS you will be trained to do your own inspections at this location.

Find out more about the scheme (opens in a new tab)

You should know

It takes some time to complete a PHEATS registration for a new address. You can continue to apply for certificates while your application is incomplete.

Before you start:

  • read the guidance (opens in a new tab)
  • make a site plan as explained in the guidance
  • decide what commodities you plan to do inspections for
  • identify who will take on the role of person responsible and choose at least one authorised person
  • make sure you have unique email addresses for the person responsible and all the authorised people so they can agree to the terms and conditions
! Warning Once you begin your application you must complete it without saving or exiting. Make sure you have everything you need to complete the registration before you begin.
