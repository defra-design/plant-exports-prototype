Get your plant export certified and inspected

Use this service to apply for a plant health certificate (a phytosanitary certificate) to:

  • export plants and plant products, seeds, grain, bulbs and potatoes
  • export used farm machinery

You can also arrange an inspection of:

  • the crop your plants or plant products have come from
  • the soil your plants are growing in

You'll need a Government Gateway account - if you don't already have one, you can set one up.

Start now

Before you start

Check the rules about your commodity set by the equivalent of Defra in the importing country. You may not need to apply for a certificate.

Do not apply earlier than 14 days from your planned export date as your certificate will expire.

There's a different process for exporting wood, wood packaging or products made from wood.

You can also apply by filling in the forms and sending them to APHA by email.
