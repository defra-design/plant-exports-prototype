Get plant health export certificates

Use this service to apply for a certificate

  • to export plants and fresh produce, processed plant products, seeds, grain, bulbs and potatoes
  • to export used farm machinery
  • to re-forward (re-export) fruit, cut flowers, seeds and seed potatoes

You can also apply for a certificate of conformity.

You can also arrange:

  • soil sampling
  • a growing season inspection

You'll need a Government Gateway account - if you don't already have one, you can set one up.

If you are applying to export fruit, vegetables or cut flowers, you may have the option to apply under the plant health exports audited trader scheme (PHEATS).

Start now

Before you start

Check the rules about your commodity set by the equivalent of Defra in the importing country. You may not need to apply for a certificate.

Do not apply earlier than 14 days from your planned export date as your certificate will expire.

There's a different process for exporting wood, wood packaging or products made from wood.

You can also apply by filling in the forms and sending them to APHA by email.
